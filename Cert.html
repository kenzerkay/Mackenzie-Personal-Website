Certification in College Teaching

About the Program

The Certification in College Teaching (CCT) is a formal certification program at Michigan State University that allows students to learn and develop teaching strategies. This program focuses on completing 5 core competencies and a Teaching Philosophy to show development of key teaching principles.

5 Core competencies:

  • Core Competency 1: Developing Discipline-Related Teaching Strategies
  • Core Competency 2: Creating Effective Learning Environments
  • Core Competency 3: Incorporating Technology in your Teaching
  • Core Competency 4: Understanding the University Context
  • Core Competency 5: Assessing Student Learning

Core Competency 1: Developing Discipline-Related Teaching Strategies

Description of Competency

Competency 1 focuses on developing discipline specific pedagogical strategies and usually some type of formal course is taken to fulfill this requirement To complete this competency, I took ISE 870: Teaching College Science in Spring 2025. This course focused on scientifically motivated pedagogy for teaching science coursed in particular.

Artifacts from Course

The artifact I have decided to highlight from this class is the presentation portion of our final project which was given in class during one our of ISE 870 sessions. A significant portion of our final for the course was creating a mock syllabus for a course. We worked in pairs to complete this and my partner and I designed an introductory level course for first years in the astronomy major. In addition to creating the syllabus, we had to design a specific lesson from the course using backwards course design. My partner and I did this for the "what is light?" portion of the course where we propose students completing a spectroscopy lab during class. We had to lay out specifics for what the lesson would look like and how students would be assessed on this topic.

Reflection

Overall, this class was extremely beneficial in having me reflect on different pedagogical strategies
