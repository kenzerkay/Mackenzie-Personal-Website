Certification in College Teaching (Work In Progress)

About the Program

The Certification in College Teaching (CCT) is a formal certification program at Michigan State University that gives graduate students and postdocs the opportunity to learn about and develop teaching strategies. This program focuses on completing 5 core competencies and a teaching philosophy to show development of key teaching principles.

5 Core Competencies:

  • Core Competency 1: Developing Discipline-Related Teaching Strategies
  • Core Competency 2: Creating Effective Learning Environments
  • Core Competency 3: Incorporating Technology in your Teaching
  • Core Competency 4: Understanding the University Context
  • Core Competency 5: Assessing Student Learning

For each of these competencies I will include artifacts and materials developed from the activities I used to completed these competencies.

Core Competency 1: Developing Discipline-Related Teaching Strategies

Description of Competency

Competency 1 focuses on exploring pedagogical techniques for teaching in a given field. There are a wide variety of different educational strategies to employ but their effectiveness can vary based on topic and audience. As a computational astrophysics who is interested in higher education it is incredibly important to keep that context in mind when thinking about teaching practices. That is why I took ISE 870: Teaching College Science in Spring 2025 which is a course at Michigan State that focuses on the topic and audience I want to gear myself towards. In this semester long-course we examined many different teaching strategies including modeling, argument driven inquiry, and backwards design, and would practice implementing these techniques by creating mock lessons for classes at the college level.

Artifact from ISE 870

I have included the syllabus for ISE 870 to highlight some of the topics we discussed and activities we completed during this class. The main focus of the course was to allow us to explore different teaching practices and for every teaching practice we learned we had to plan how we could implement that into a real-world classroom setting. Most of the class periods were focused on active collaboration with our peers where we would discuss the pros and cons of each teaching strategy and how we could implement these strategies in our own (sometimes hypothetical) classrooms. We tackled the many other discussion topics such as the difficulty in teaching large classes and how to engage students at different levels of knowledge in the subject matter.

Material Developed During ISE 870

A significant portion of the course was spent working on a final project that incorporated many of the teaching strategies we learned throughout the semester. We created a mock syllabus for a course and designed a specific lesson using backwards course design. My partner and I designed an introductory level course for first years in the astronomy major and used backwards design to create a hands-on lesson for the topic of spectra. I have chosen to highlight the final presentation that my partner and I gave for this project. This presentation neatly summarizes our course and the specific lesson we designed.

Reflection on Disciple-Related Teaching Strategies

Overall, this class was extremely beneficial in having me reflect on different pedagogical practices. This course gave me the freedom and knowledge to explore teaching techniques and strategize on how to implement them in a real world classroom setting. The content of ISE 870 also incorporated ideas for many of the other competencies in the certification process.

Core Competency 2: Creating Effective Learning Environments

Description of Competency

Competency 2 focuses on creating inclusive and equitable spaces where students are able to grow and learn effectively. I completed this competency during the Certification in College Teaching Institute in the summer of 2025, which is a professional development seminar series aimed at helping students completed the Certification process.

Core Competency 3: Incorporating Technology in your Teaching

Description of Competency

Competency 3 focuses on incorporating technology into your teaching practices. I completed this competency during the CCTI in the summer of 2025.

Artifacts from Course

For Competency 3 I have included an instructions for an activity form this particular professional seminar. The activity was to take everyday items and discuss how we might incorporate them into a lecture in a disciple. My group obtained a rubber band and discussed how we could use this potentially discuss tension forces............... I think the really important revelation that I had during this activity is to expand my definition of "technology in the classroom". Using the rubber bands as "technology" inspired me to try to be more creative with how objects can be used for educational purposes.

Reflection

When reflecting on the broad category of "technology in the classroom", it is apparent to me that almost anything can be an instructional aid with just a little bit of inspiration and creatively.
